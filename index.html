Yam Pepper Soup Recipe

Yam pepper soup is a Nigerian dish made using soft white puna yam. It is necessary for the yam to be soft. The soup is common amongst the South-Eastern part of Nigeria and some of the ingredients required to make it are ehuru, chili pepper, varieties of meat, salt and scent leaf. These ingredients are added when the yam is soft such that it start to form porridge.

Recipe Information

Prep Time: 15mins

Cook Time: 30mins

Servings: 4

Category: Soup

Cuisine: Nigerian

Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

  • 500g soft white puna yam
  • 2-3 pieces of ehuru (calabash nutmeg)
  • 2-3 tablespoons of ground chili pepper
  • Assorted meat (beef, goat, or chicken)
  • Salt to taste
  • Scent leaf (Nchuanwu) for garnish

Instructions

  1. Peel and cut the yam into chunks, then boil in water until soft.
  2. In a separate pot, add the assorted meat, ehuru, and chili pepper. Season with salt and cook until the meat is tender.
  3. Once the yam is soft, add it to the pot with the meat and mash it to your desired consistency.
  4. Stir in the scent leaf and let it simmer for a few minutes.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with additional scent leaf if desired.

Notes

- Ensure the yam is very soft for the best texture.
- Adjust the amount of chili pepper based on your spice preference.
- This recipe can be adapted with different types of meat or fish as desired.
